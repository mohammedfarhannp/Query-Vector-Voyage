Palestinian Shawarma Recipe
-----
Palestinian shawarma is a flavorful and aromatic meat dish, typically made with marinated chicken or beef. To prepare it, start by slicing boneless chicken thighs or beef thinly. For the marinade, mix together plain yogurt, white vinegar, olive oil, crushed garlic, ground cardamom, allspice, cinnamon, black pepper, paprika, cumin, salt, and a touch of lemon juice. Coat the meat thoroughly with this marinade and let it rest for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, in the refrigerator. After marinating, cook the meat on a hot grill or in a large pan over medium-high heat until it becomes tender and slightly crispy at the edges. Traditionally, the meat is stacked on a vertical rotisserie and shaved off slowly, but at home, pan-cooking works well. To serve, warm a pita bread or markouk bread, spread a thin layer of tahini sauce or garlic paste (toum), and add the shawarma meat. Optionally, add pickled cucumbers, sliced tomatoes, fresh parsley, and a drizzle of sumac or lemon juice. Roll the bread tightly and toast it on a hot pan for a minute on each side to make the exterior crisp. Palestinian shawarma is often served with fries on the side or stuffed inside the sandwich itself. The result is a savory, tangy, and satisfying meal.
